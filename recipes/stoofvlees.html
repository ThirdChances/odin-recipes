Stoofvlees (beef-stew) with fries

Description

Flemish Stew, known in Dutch as stoofvlees or stoverij and in French as carbon(n)ade à la flamande is a Flemish beef (or pork) and onion stew popular in Belgium, the Netherlands and French Flanders, made with beer and mustard spread on bread, seasoning can include bay leaf, thyme, clove and juniper berries.

As a staple of Belgian cuisine, recipes will vary between regions, and between households.
Swapping bread for gingerbread or speculaas, using extra condiments, like Sirop de Liège, and using different kinds of beer.
Common beers for this dish include Oud bruin (Old Brown Beer), Brune Abbey beer and Flanders red. Just before serving, a small amount of cider or wine vinegar and either brown sugar or red currant jelly are sometimes added.

Carbonade is often accompanied by french fries with mayonaise (Belgian mayonaise! believe me when i say the is a big difference), boiled potatoes or stoemp.
It is widely available in restaurants and friteries in Belgium and the Netherlands.

Ingredients

  • 2 big onions
  • 1kg of beef(chuck roast)
  • Salt
  • Brown belgian beer (2 bottles)
  • A couple of laurel leaves
  • Thyme
  • 1 clove
  • 2 tablespoons Sirop de Liege
  • 1 slice of brown bread
  • 2 tablespoons of sharp mustard
  • Vinegar
  • Parsley
  • Salt & pepper
  • Fries or potatoes cut into fries

Steps

  1. Clean and cut the onions. Cut the meat into cubes about 3x3x2 cm
  2. Sear the meat in small portions. Don't overfill the pan! Add salt and pepper to taste.
  3. In a larger pot stew your onion until soft but not brown. Add your meat to the pot.
  4. Put your beer in the pan you used to sear your meat and heat up your beer slightly. Stir to get all the baked bits that stick to your pan into the beer.
    Add the beer to your meat as soon as it cooks.
  5. Bundle up your laurel, thyme and parsley with a piece of rope and add it to your stew
  6. Add the cloves and the sirop de Liege
  7. Take the bread and spread the mustard royaly on the bread. Put it in the pot with the mustard side down
  8. Let it simmer for a minimum of 2 hours (the longer the better) while stirring now and then.
  9. Only put on the lid when the sauce has the right thickness. Then add a little vinegar and maybe some salt and pepper.
  10. Bake your fries and serve together with some Belgian mayonaise. Enjoy!
